# The Eldritch Horror in Apt C

The Neighbors keep asking me if I’m okay. How I’m doing. I don’t know what to say. I tell them I’m doing, alright…

My apartment is full of demons. The glowing lights spill out into the hallway. The villagers usually pretend not to see. Sometimes they come banging their torches on the door. I don’t host many visitors that aren’t trying to burn me at the stake, but when I do I tell them, “No One’s home!”

It isn’t exactly a lie. My welcome mat is a circle of salt, the plaque above it reads “Abandon Hope,” or else its something like “Hell, Sweet, Hell” 

My mother never held me as a child. I was unclean. When she bathed me, I would kick and splash as I whined, “I want my mommy. I want to go home.”

She would look down on me with the calm condescension only a mother could have. “I _am_ your mommy. You _are_ home.” Completely oblivious to the venom dripping from her words. As if they were meant to comfort me. Do not try to comfort me, I am inconsolable. 

I bathe almost daily now, yet my hands are never clean. This place is disgusting. The World Itself makes my flesh crawl. It is dirt under my fingernails. I load the dishwasher and everything comes out dirtier than when I put it in. The rats laugh at me through the walls. I lick my knives clean and clog the food disposal with their bones. 

Most nights I sleep on the couch. It is an uncomfortable rest. The little love seat was only meant for two. But down here in the wreckage it’s you, and me, and the Devil makes three. It’s a cold world, we fight for the blanket. I bruise your head, you strike my heel.

The cockroaches swarm all over me anyways. Burrowing into my skin, nesting in my hair, writhing in my ass crack. I’ve never actually seen them, but they are present in the broad gesture if not in the details. _Maybe they’re in my veins._

One night I had just about enough. I split my wrists open and watched them skitter out. The toxins flowed out of me and left crimson stains on the carpet. Instead of breaking out the raid or taking off my shoe, I picked one up, as calmly as I could. Held him like a newborn baby. Pinched between my index and thumb I watched him squirm.

“Please, have mercy,” he pled in his palmetto tongue, legs clasped together in prayer.

“Mary held God in her arms,” I told him. “And He was crying.”

God never answered my prayers either. But I am more merciful than He, so I crushed Beezlebub between my fingertips. He kicked his hairy limbs and gnashed his jaws in vain. I popped him like a zit.

The human rodents ran for their puny lives at the cloven heels of my feet. My manic laughter drowned out their prayers as I stomped on the fleeing villagers. The old lady in the apartment beneath me did not appreciate this. She was tapping Morse code through the floor, likely with a broomstick. We were pressed together like sardines. A Tower of bodies piled high, spiraling toward the heavens where a dark and hungry god awaits our arrival with a gaping, carnivorous smile.

“DIE, SINNER, DIE!” I kept on purging on my righteous crusade. “I AM THE FLAMING SWORD OF EDEN!”

I hit the walls and yelled real loud. Ceremorphosis had come for me at last. My apartment was a cocoon birthing the new god. Melting away the flesh and clumps of hair. The tadpole in my brain took everything I had to give. It took away my friends. It took my will to live. It even took my teeth and spat them out in the kitchen sink.

Their bloated bodies wafted in the stagnant water.

I shed off my human skin and put it in the closet with the rest of my disguises. I emerged, flayed naked. Bleeding. Fully formed in God’s own image. 

Life and death was a whim at my command.

“You live. You die. You die. You live,” the Virgin Mary of pestilence’s cunt was splitting open with the soft meat of divine life “Life sucks, Dickhead. DIE, MOTHERFUCKER, DIE!”

Japanese business men looked toward the skies in terror. Even Mothra could not save them, though she tried. Buzzing around me with the Air Force fighter jets. Their missiles were like bug bites to me. I swatted them all down like flies.

“I HAVE BECOME DEATH! DESTROYER OF WORLDS!” I roared, blood howling through the carnage. I bit the heads off their heroes with a satisfying crunch. 

The walls had teeth. The World was closing in, chewing me up and spiting me out. 

“FUCK! I CAN’T TAKE IT ANYMORE!” The neighbors hate it when I scream at the Sun, begging it not to rise. They laugh at me, or else their screaming “SHUT THE FUCK UP!”

I can’t tell. My love language is in clicks and hisses. I try not to talk to the voices in the walls anyways.

I passed out there on the kitchen floor, as the Sun rose despite my efforts. Among the dead. One more corpse for the pyre. 

The next day, I got up and went to work. Because that’s what the normal people do. My human suit was a little loose around the eye sockets. I avoided the neighbors’ glares as I stumbled down the hallway. _I leave a trail of blood in my wake, everywhere I go._ I could feel the judgment in their stares breathing down my neck. They’re sharpening their pitchforks, I’m sure of it. _You’re not like us. You don’t belong here. You should go home._
 
But I am home. I live somewhere between the second and sixth rib.

I hadn’t eaten all day. Too many people around. I cannot let the mortal men watch me feed. Still, I could taste the blood in my mouth anyways. 
The sun was too bright. The birds were too loud. The flowers reeked. I couldn’t think of anything but returning to my lair and slumbering for a thousand years. Until the cities crumbled into the sea. Until the age of man was no more. Until the heat death of the universe.

I slept for 4 hours.

Spent most of the next day jerking off. 

It had become my religion, I was ritualistic about it. Lit some candles. Anointed thyself in unholy oils. Even summoned a human sacrifice. The ancient goetias called for a virgin, but whores work just as well once you’ve acquired the taste. Less likely to be missed too. 

I had become rather fond of this one Argentine prostitute. Victoria de los Angeles Rojas. If I was god she was my Priestess. I called her Vic the Spic, while she worshipped at my altar. And I would bring her lower.

She was on her hands and knees, panties in her mouth, fulfilling her life’s purpose as my foot rest.

“Hey, Vic?” I inquired, picking at a scab as I scrolled through porn on my 60’ inch television screen. Nothing could satisfy the itch. The outer gods had not given me the cosmic energies necessary to do much else, but scratch until I bled. Someone had to break the silence and since her mouth was full, I figured it might as well be me.

“Yes, Master?” She spat out her thong to reply to me. An act that would earn her slightly less lashes than not speaking when spoken to.

“What did you study in Argentina again?”

“Political Science.”

“And what are you now?”

“A slave.” There was a sigh of detachment in her that I found disrespectful. So I struck her with the belt. “_Your_ slave, Master.”

“You want to pay for another hour then?”

I couldn’t afford another hour. I was out of time. Luckily, I had some oregano in the cupboard.

I _really_ liked Vic. More than I’ve liked anyone. So I put her on the shelf.

I made empandas for the homeless guys at the bus stop. The choosers complained that the meat tasted kind of strange. They begged me for $2. It’s always $2 dollars these days. They use to just ask for change. Now they want money. The economy must be getting worse because the bums aren’t getting any fatter. They taste the same either way. Like home.

I still have a home to go back to, for now, at least. I live in the Uncanny Valley. I show up as a video glitch on the neighbours' security cameras. My shibboleths scare the locals. Children tell ghost stories about me by campfires. 

 “He wears the skin of his victims and dances naked in the woods!” I don’t even know how to dance, the rest I plead the fifth. I shall take my secrets to the grave, like the government agents that never return from their welfare checks. 

Are you even listening?

Do you understand what I’m saying? Can you illiterate fucks not read between the lines? I don’t write horror, I write sins and tragedies. It’s all just word salad. I’m trying to eat healthy, but my mouth is full of blood and foam.

Watch my apartment while I’m gone, will you? I don’t think the feds are doing a good enough job. I hid the key in the cryptic symbolism I left in your dreams. Let the skeletons out of the closet for some fresh air. If you hear any strange sounds coming from the drains, the demons are probably just hungry. I haven’t fed them in awhile. 

Sorry about the mess. Don’t bother cleaning the blood splatter off the wall, leave it as a warning to whoever comes after me. Tell them they just missed me by a _minute._
