# The Danse Macabre

[Back Home](/)


	Every day I wake up in a different carcass. These flailing bodies still breathe, eat, and shit. They laugh and they cry. Sometimes they dance. They do all the things that the living bodies do. But they are dead. They just don’t know it yet.
	
    I must have been alive once, I figure. All dead things were once alive. Its part of the routine and everything steps in tandem with the routine. Wake up, stumble out of bed, pirouette to the shower, brush my teeth, drink coffee, eat toast, running late, sprint to the bus stop, cross the street—Get hit by the bus. It happens everyday. With couplet consistency. But I’ve always been dead. I came into this world dying and I have been struggling for air ever since. Over and over and over again.
	
    I probably had a body of my own, once too. One that looked like me, smelled like me, one that I could see looking back in the mirror. A form to bind my soul… Is that what I am? A soul. All my vessels seem to think they have a soul, but they don’t seem to agree on what it is or where it resides. I don’t think they mean me though. I am just an unwelcome visitor, I am a ghost haunting the attic of their body. I am the mice in their walls. I am cancer metastasizing in their brain. They rarely acknowledge me but everyone knows I’m there. They all know me. They all know my name. I am Death, and I am immortal.
	
    Tonight, I am a goth teenager sneaking into the local cemetery to get high and write poetry. The branches above are strewn in clumps of Spanish moss, the guts of spirits swaying like hanged men. Bats flutter drunkenly through the night air, chanting to Satan. All devils are here now. Banshee and Poe had come to bathe in the full moonlight. They held each other down here in the dark places, reveling in death and lamenting their lives.
	
        “Change and Decay in all around I see,” the young poet recited, clutching his black book of spells, words which could only be his own. “O thou who changes not, abide with me.”
	
    I abode.

	Banshee shuffled the tarot deck. Her hands were fluid motion. Quick successive cuts and splits cut through the strings of fate. She drew three cards face down, each with intention, as if she could manifest the correct card by simply believing it to be true. She fanned them out between us, placing her hand on the first card. 
	
        “Spirits we beseech you, tell us our fortune. Will Poe ever be a great poet.” She flipped the card over. The Fool. She snorted with laughter.
	    
        “Hey, I think you’re pretty not bad, Poe. But the spirits have spoken.”
	
        “Shut up, Bans. What does it mean?”
	
        “It’s change,” She lowered her voice, taking on a new found gravity. “You’re going on a journey, one that might be more dangerous than you realize. You might not come back if you aren’t careful.”
	
        “Uhh… my asshole dad’s making me get a job. You think my boss is going to kill me?”
	
        “Probably. Work seems stupid, that’s why I’m just going to be an artist. There’s an easy life. Plus they want you to get up early, and sunlight is bad for my complexion.”
	
    Banshee pulled a cigarette out of her purse and lit it. She took one long drag and handed it to my host. He graciously accepted, noting the black smear of lipstick as he put it to his own lips. She pulled out a pocket mirror and gazed lovingly inside. She could see her reflection in it, she said, because the glass wasn’t real silver.
	
    Banshee turned over the next card. A skeleton in black and shinning armor sat proudly on a pale horse. One of my more flattering depictions.
	
        “Death?” Poe coughed, passing her back the blunt. I realize now it wasn’t a cigarette after all. A shame, cancer is such a beautiful death.
	
        “You scared,” she said poking him. Poe blushed. She laughed. “Death doesn’t mean death, idiot. It’s like… change or whatever.”
	
        “I thought the fool was change?”
	
        “Death is change too. Its like a transformation. New beginnings. It’s not bad… just… I don’t know, this is stupid. You wanna do something else?”
	
    Poe never got his final card, but I told him his fortune anyways. 
	
        “Ruin.” Every story ends with ruin. Happily ever after is just a story left incomplete. 
	
        “There is beauty in the ruins,” Poe said, scrawling a new spell. “For those with the eyes to see.” 
	
    The dead have no such luxuries. 
	
        “What?” Banshee was setting up her Ouija board. Five candles burned in the shape of a pentagram on the spirit eye blanket. Lavender and Pumkinpie didn’t quite paint the spooky atmosphere she wanted but you can’t have a séance without candles. And the attention to detail was appreciated.

	    “Hello, spirit world,” we chanted in unison, black fingernails pressed together in a heart on the panchette. “Is anyone there?”
	
    All around tombstones stared up at us enviously, wanting to talk but saying nothing. I knew there was no judgment in their gaze, or their silence, only the deep longing that only dead things can feel. But still Poe felt judged and refused to look them in the eye.
	
        “Yo, spirits, it’s me ya girl Bans.” They laughed. Their fingers moved from the spirit board to each other’s bodies. Light as a feather, stiff as a board. Tongues and lips wrapped into a passionate kiss. Neither of them thought to end the séance with so much as a goodbye. They left the door open, so the dead could watch.
	
    They had chosen a proper haunted graveyard for their trysts. It was over a century old. Small and unmaintained. An intimate place where they would never be disturbed by the living. Just a few blocks off their sad, suburban cul de sac. Broken, moss ridden stoneheads jutted out the damp earth in all directions. Bodies buried haphazardly as they ran out of room and needed more space for my handy work. Death is an always expanding business after all.
	
    This cemetery belonged to the two of them for all practical purposes. Them, and the dearly departed. It had many residents but only a few visitors. Bans could not stand to see the hallowed ground forsaken. So, she would come down here with her boyfriend and they’d play their occultist games. And drink and smoke and laugh and fuck, but she always tried to leave it nicer than she found it. She would pick up trash, clear the overgrowth from headstones, and leave flowers where she saw fit. All were remembered, all were loved and accounted for. All of this was lost on Poe. But I saw, it was recorded.
	
    Many of the names had faded by then. Poe was too focused on Banshee’s hand in his, and her tits on the other hand, to notice any of the names rotting in pieces around us. I recognized a few of my old haunts. It’s my job to remember. To write the names in the Book of the Dead. Someone has got to do it. I insure they are seen. I make sure they are remembered. I am a good accountant tallying up the assets and liabilities on their balance sheet. My records are flawless.
	
     Catlynn Fitzgerald 1925-1932. Missing, presumed dead. And they presumed right. She fell in a well. She could hear her parents call for her but by then she had spent too long screaming to call back. A tragedy to some, a dark joke to others. James Murphy 1889-1924. Shot over a card game. Sometimes lady luck smiles on you, and sometimes she spits in your face. “Big” Jim Dwyer. His stone read “Loving Father”, but he was a hard man. Never told anyone he loved them. He did, but didn’t know how to form the words. The cancer ate everything. Chair bound, shaky hands barely able to lift his cigarette to his mouth, he found the words. And his firstborn son leaned in to hear them. But now in his final moments the words came out a hoarse whisper. They died on his lips.  
	
        I told him, “I heard you.”
	
    But I don’t think he heard me. They never do. 
	
    The dead make for good company, once you get past the lonliness. I’ve played my part in countless kodokushis. In Aokigahara, many sad and lonely people have cultivated a forest to my companionship. There are signs, telling them to turn back. Think it through. But like diligent gardeners they come to water my leaves with their tears. They cry that they don’t want to die alone and I make sure they never do. I stay with them until the bitter end. Until the rope snaps. But still, no one ever hangs around too long.
	At least Poe wouldn’t die alone. He was right where God intended him to be with the love of his short little life. Banshee had told him that their souls were connected, and he felt that in his heart to be true. Poe took out his pocket knife and began carving into a tree. The darkness made for clumsy work but still their love was immortalized upon oaken flesh.
	
        Bans + Poe 4 Eva.
	
    Poe wasn’t his “real” name, of course. His dead name was something much more mundane. A name his parents loving bestowed on him after looking at several lists of top 100 baby names and picking the first one their friends and family hadn’t already used. Banshee had given him his truename because he was Edgar Alan Poe in his past life. She could read it in his aura. Or so she said. Banshee said a lot of things like that. She would swear she was a vampire and needed to drink blood. Whether she actually believed these things I cannot say. I haven’t met her yet, I’ve only seen her through Poe’s eyes.
	
    Poe went along with it because he thought it was hot when she cut him and fed. She said his artist blood nourished her and she would eagerly lap it up off of his skin as they become a tangle of grasping hands seeking out whatever comfort they could caress. Like moths to the flame they sought out something warm, but the whole world is kindling. It is dry and dead and flammable. Poe felt something warm tickle his foot, and all too late remembered what his mother had told him about playing with matches, and leaving a candle unattended. He stumbled back and spilled his final thoughts upon a slab of granite. The last thing he heard was the banshee’s wail. 
	
    Two lovers went down to the graveyard that night. But only one ever came back.
	
    Some say I’m cruel and heartless because I take whatever partners I please. Their mothers and fathers, sons and daughters, all dance with me eventually. They are all just bodies in motion, as am I, and we cannot stop moving our feet to the sound of the beat. We waltz in abandoned parking lots and out onto the streets. Others dance at home alone where no one can see them stumble and fall. But the dance must go on.
	Now, I am sitting in my car staring out at the Cumberland river. Its muddy brown waters leave something to be desired. The air reeks of sewage. The night birds are crying just off in the tree line. Hank Williams is on the stereo commiserating with that lonesome whippoorwill. My host has a half full bottle of Jim Bean clenched in his fist and a 1967 Colt 45 in the passenger seat. Becca won’t return his calls. She blocked him on everything. She even took the kids. A fresh break, clean slate, she said. He had gotten through to her only once, but she hung up while he was still talking.
	
    It’s over, I know. I couldn’t say where it all went wrong. But I know it’s over, and so does he. They look so happy in the photographs my host desperately scrolls through. He’s searching for some sign or pattern that he will never find. An indication of where everything fell apart. We are all just bodies in motion, waiting for something to collide with. Our end was written into the beginning. When you take the long view as I do, you can see the trajectory of any given path, but all roads lead to the same destination eventually. The truth is if he had been more assertive with his wants and needs, she would have left him sooner. Maybe it wouldn’t have hurt so much then. She might have stayed longer if he made more money, or was a better lover, or was half an inch taller. Or if he had proposed on a Tuesday. But in the end, we’d still be sitting right here, on the same damn bridge looking out at the same shit water. Fate is a cruel mistress, and she never loved you, so fuck her anyways.
	
        “I loved her so much,” he said aloud to himself. He saw no point in saying the rest but I could hear his thoughts all the same. “But maybe love just wasn’t enough. Maybe I wasn’t enough.”
	
        “She said I was broken. She said I was a ghost in our marriage. A FUCKING GHOST! She said I had no passion. I’ll show that FUCKING WHORE passion.”
	
    He downed the rest of his cheap whiskey in large thirsty gulps and threw the bottle to the fish. We’d be sleeping together tonight, whiskey dick or not.
	
        “Eight years. eight looong years.” He could never get those years back. She had stolen them from him. It was too late to start again. He didn’t want to reset the clock. He was already out of time, he thought. He didn’t want to learn to love again. All his loves and ambitions were tied up with her.
	
        “And she just walked out my life. After everything we’ve been through? After everything, she’s just going to become a ghost to me?”
	
    My host’s rage burned through me. He was carrying a torch too hot to handle. Images of her fucking someone else in our bed played on repeat. He stopped the tape, rewound it. Freeze frame on every gritty detail. Again and again. He played out the same scene in the theater of his mind like an actor rehearsing his lines.
	
    I could no longer see where he ended and I began. Just the all consuming fire of his anger. Is this what you call passion? He was a wounded animal clawing out at any perceived threat. Hissing and biting at the same hand who had come to comfort him. We wrestled for control.
	
        “Don’t do anything stupid,” I told him. “Just go home. Sleep it off. Wake up new.”
	
    But he didn’t listen. They never want to listen, they just want to dance. So my host pulled back onto the road gnashing his teeth and hitting the dashboard with his fist, trying to shake me off him.
	
        “If shes fucking him, I swear to GOD Imma shoot them both!”
	
        “Don’t do it. Think of the kids.” I whispered over his shoulder.
	
        “They’re probably not even mine, that fucking whore…”
	    
        “You should watch the road. Pull over. Turn off the engine. Get out the car and walk.”
	
        “I’mma kill her and everything’s going to be alright.” He turned the volume up, his foot on the petal tapping to his own dirge. All gas, no breaks. “I’m going to tie that bitch up and lock her in the trunk. Imma slit her lying throat. I’m going to rip out her heart like she did mine. I'm going to stuff her and put her head next to the dear on my mantle. And then I’m—”
	
    And then he hit the railing and plummeted to the water beneath him. The Cumberland embraced us with loving arms and Hank Williams soothed us to sleep. Everything slipped away. And I woke up new.
	
        “Drunk driver?” I now say.
	
        “Nah,” my partner replies, “This asshole knew what he was doing.”
	
    He had cut across three lanes of traffic to drown down there in the muck. A bullet on its marked trajectory, yet fired at random. Impulse had consumed him without so much as a second thought. As if some spirit had possessed him in the moment. A rabbit that zigged when it meant to zag. The Devil took the wheel and showed him an exit. 
	
        “So, suicide then?” Sometimes you lead, and sometimes you follow.
	
        “Yeah,” he shakes his head. He’s already bopping to the tune. “Fucking pathetic, ain’t it?”
	
    A voice crackles through the radio static and I’m off to another job.
	
    Time ebbs and flows day after day, corpse after corpse. The endless shuffle of the daily grind. I dance at weddings and I dance at funerals. I boogie to disco. I jive to the Blues. Always moving. Always grooving. Always trying to keep up with the tempo. I am murder. I am disease. I am an electrical fire. I am a drug induced heart attack. I am everything you hate. I am a elderly woman freezing on a park bench. I am a child playing in the streets of Gaza. I am unwashed hands. I am unprotected sex. I am the fourth horseman in every no horse town. God might have rested on the Seventh day, but I have busy feet, places to be and people to meet. So on I dance.
	
    Tonight, I am safe and warm. I am alive. I can feel my heart pumping blood, a drum beating in my chest. It has been so long since I have tasted life. So long since I could remember hunger or thirst. And soon it will return. As it always does.
	It is dark and cramped in here. Everytime I try to stretch my legs it feels like the whole world is kicking back against me. I want to live but I am stuck. I am a prisoner trapped in flesh. Flesh that will betray me. Flesh made of wants and needs. Flesh that bruises and fails. The spirit is willing, but the flesh is so very, very weak.
	
    Something in the cosmos shifts, and I become disorientated. Everything is motion, tossing and pulling me. Wave upon wave breaks against the shore. And I can’t breathe. God, I can’t breathe. Why can’t I breathe? I am drowning. I struggle and struggle but the air never finds my lungs. I see no light at the end of the tunnel. No hope. Only meaningless nothingness, darkness eternal. A void staring back at me. I cry out for help but nobody is listening. Someone, anyone, can you hear me?! I am a rabid beast howling at the moon-- I am tooth and claw, I wail and rattle my chains but no one hears me. I am a ghost wrapped in decaying flesh.
	And then the light comes suddenly, blindingly. Shapes struggle to form in my half shut eyes. Just bodies of blobs dancing to some unknown rhythm. All these strange sounds and smells penetrate me from all directions, bumping and stumbling, stepping on my toes. 
	
    I hear a blood churning roar. 
	
        “PUSH!” and everything electric slides.
	
    I am frightened. I don’t know where I am or what’s happening. I perceive the world through touch and smell, but all around me is noise. I feel wet and slimy. And my hands flail out in search for something but grasp at nothing. Foreign hands grope and claw at the new flesh. They poke and prod and put me on display. It’s eat or be eaten and I am just compost in training. The world keeps spinning me around, everything moving so fast, swirling and twirling from moment to moment. Everything changing in constant flux.
	
    Until, I am wrapped up and held tightly. I am safe and secure. The rumbling chaos begins to fade away until there is nothing but a rhythmic titter patter tapping.
	
        “Look,” a voice from heaven cries out, “It’s raining for you.”
	
    I open my eyes. Translucent shapes come into focus, glazed behind some kind of wet film. The sky cracks open in a Wilhelm scream. And as I peer into the face of The Great Something, The Something stares right back at me. It’s eyes are crystal balls, futures are told within them. It places its thumb to my warm cheek and wipes something away.
	
        “Shhhh, don’t cry. That’s just the angels dancing,” the voice sings in soprano, barely audible above the thunderous choir. It is soft and kind. And it names me. Gives me form. Binds me to this world. “I love you.”
	
    In this moment, I don’t know what the words mean, but something in them brings me great joy and comfort. I am glad I am alive. I close my eyes and wake up in a different carcass.
