Back Home

The Neighbors keep asking me if I’m okay. How I’m doing. I don’t know what to say. I tell them I’m doing, alright…

My apartment is full of demons. The glowing lights spill out into the hallway. The villagers usually pretend not to see. Sometimes they come banging their torches on the door. I don’t host many visitors that aren’t trying to burn me at the stake, but when I do I tell them, “No One’s home!”

It isn’t exactly a lie. My welcome mat is a circle of salt, the plaque above it reads “Abandon Hope,” or else its something like “Hell, Sweet, Hell”

My mother never held me as a child. I was unclean. When she bathed me, I would kick and splash as I whined, “I want my mommy. I want to go home.”

She would look down on me with the calm condescension only a mother could have. “I am your mommy. You are home.” Completely oblivious to the venom dripping from her words. As if they were meant to comfort me. Do not try to comfort me, I am inconsolable.

I bathe almost daily now, yet my hands are never clean. This place is disgusting. The World Itself makes my flesh crawl. It is dirt under my fingernails. I load the dishwasher and everything comes out dirtier than when I put it in. The rats laugh at me through the walls. I lick my knives clean and clog the food disposal with their bones.

Most nights I sleep on the couch. It is an uncomfortable rest. The little love seat was only meant for two. But down here in the wreckage it’s you, and me, and the Devil makes three. It’s a cold world, we fight for the blanket. I bruise your head, you strike my heel.

A Tower of bodies piled high, spiraling toward the heavens where a dark and hungry god awaits our arrival with a gaping, carnivorous smile.

“DIE, SINNER, DIE!” I kept on purging on my righteous crusade. “I AM THE FLAMING SWORD OF EDEN!”

They’re sharpening their pitchforks, I’m sure of it. You’re not like us. You don’t belong here. You should go home. But I am home. I live somewhere between the second and sixth rib.
